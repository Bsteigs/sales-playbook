Sales Playbook
Abacus Group
Stage
Sections
Never do this
  • No monologues over 30 seconds
  • No early solutioning
  • No pitch before Implication
  • No deck dumping
  • No competing on tools
  • No AI/SOC/IR unless tied to pain
  • Never ask for budget directly
  • Never leave without next step booked
  • Silence is a weapon
  • The buyer must verbalize risk themselves
Deal Killers
  • "What's your budget?"
  • "Are you happy with your MSP?"
  • "What tools are you using?"
  • "How soon are you switching?"
  • "Who makes the final decision?"
  • "Why did you take this call?"
  • "Let me show you how we do it."
  • "What's not working?"
  • "Can we circle back later?"
  • "Does that make sense?"
Emergency Resets
  • "Let me pause us and realign on what matters most to you."
  • "I want to pull us back to the risk you mentioned earlier."
  • "Before I go deeper, is this actually relevant to what you're solving?"
  • "I want to stay aligned with your priorities here."
Post-Call Reflection
  • What rapport did we build?
  • Business challenges they mentioned?
  • Questions I didn't fully answer?
  • Concerns or objections that surfaced?
  • Other stakeholders mentioned?
Research Triggers
  • LinkedIn — theirs and company page
  • Google News: "[Company] + news"
  • SEC filings, regulatory updates
  • New hires, departures, org changes
  • Industry publications, company blog
Execution Rules
  • Short and specific — can't feel templated
  • Don't give them homework
  • Personal reference OR something useful
  • One touchpoint per week max
  • 14+ days dark → pattern interrupt

C
Competition
Who else evaluated? What's their edge?
  • Teach — Lead with a commercial insight across peers, not what you sell.
  • Tailor — Connect the insight to their specific challenge and role.
  • Take control — Prescribe the next step. Don't wait to be asked.
  • Insight insert (one per call, after pain) — "Most MSP failures aren't technical. They're operating model failures." Always follow: "Does that resonate, or is your situation different?"
